## Shooting Philosophy

The 100-400mm IS II changes how you approach Irish light. You no longer need to strictly avoid the maximum focal length or choke the aperture down just to get a sharp image. 

1. **The 400mm Sweet Spot:** While the lens is excellent wide open, if you are pushed to the absolute maximum 400mm extension, pulling back a fraction to f/7.1 or f/8.0 tightens up micro-contrast on fine feathers. 
2. **Lean on 5-Axis Sync IS:** Because this upgraded Mark II lens supports full 5-axis Sync IS with the OM-1 Mark II body, you get up to 7.0 stops of stabilization. If a bird is completely still on a dark, overcast day, don't be afraid to drop your shutter speed to 1/160s or lower to keep your ISO pristine—the Sync IS can easily handle the hand tremors at an 800mm equivalent field of view.
3. **Action Readiness:** Keep your finger on the front dial. If a perched bird shifts its weight or looks ready to bolt, a quick scroll to 1/1600s or 1/2000s ensures you catch the flight dynamics clearly.
